Looking back over the past two years Melinda Mackay has seen her business more than triple in sales and openly acknowledges the benefit of the many FIAL programs and events she attended. Sugar Free Solutions was born out of Melinda's response to creating sugar free cake mixes for her diabetic son so he could have a birthday cake just like everyone else. Luckily, she has tapped into a growing consumer need for sugar free indulgences in local and export markets.

'FIAL's workshops have really helped me hone my business skills. The market diversification and retail workshops changed my business model. I've integrated the concepts into my strategic plan and my plan is much clearer and more targeted. I've taken the tools on board, such as how to improve my presentation skills to retail buyers. As a result of the Food Futures innovation programme, I've undertaken R&D to improve my recipes and amend my pack format to better suit my market.
 

'However, most importantly were the connections I made through the FIAL events. From being a new and small business person, I've now met many other food manufacturers, become part of the food industry and really come to understand what is needed to succeed. I've also made important practical connections such as meeting my trademark legal advisor, finding my food technologist and a new co-packer.

'At the same time I also reached out to other agencies, such as NSW T&I and Austrade who have helped me secure export orders into Hong Kong and Malaysia. I'm now planning a 15 kg food service pack for export.

'Above all, FIAL has been supportive along my business journey, offering such easy contact, relevant programs and genuine willingness to go out of their way to help me.'
 

Brisbane restauranteurs' Manolo Lopez and Silvana Calil transitioned from their Spanish restaurant to take up the challenge of developing their homemade Sangria for export, under the SKA brand. Sangria is a traditional Spanish beverage combining wine and fruit.

Spanish Boutique Beverages (SBB) is a relatively small player in the Australian beverage industry, but through their contact with FIAL were able to identify a local contract bottler to produce greater volumes with joint funding by FIAL, SBB were also able to do further product development through their partnership with Department of Agriculture & Fisheries (DAF) in Queensland.

This SME Solutions Centre (SMESC) ia an initiative that helps small to medium companies address a technical challenge whilst providing training to support the commercialisation of their product. For many businesses expertise and equipment for development is not available in-house, and as with SBB, they have to go elsewhere to get it. In this case, SBB have been working with scientists from DAF Queensland's Innovative Food Technologies RD&E 
 

(IFT) group at Coopers Plains to increase the shelf life of the full-bodied red sangria as well as expand their range into white and carbonated sangria.

'We would not be where we are today if we hadn't had the assistance of both FIAL and DAF in Queensland. They have been just superb,' he said.

Paul Burt from DAF's IFT agrees with Mr. Lopez.

'I've worked in the commercial food industry for many years and spent the past seven years in research, and this is the first time I've seen this type of funding that aims for 'real world' solutions for small to medium businesses, to solve problems and find a solution quickly.

SKA was launched in the Sydney market in early January, ahead of Melbourne later in 2015, with the aim of ultimately exporting to markets in America and Asia. 
 

A number of food companies are finding real benefits of participating in a range of FIAL market development activities. Melinda's Gluten Free Goodies registered her supplier profile in the FIAL eCatalogue, has participated in the FIAL Inbound Trade Mission in Brisbane and has been featured in the Australian Product promotion in Hong Kong high end supermarket chain Park'n Shop. While she is already successfully exporting to Asia, these activities have resulted in new retail orders.

'The opportunity to meet the International Merchandise Director from Park'n Shop face to face at the Brisbane leg of the FIAL Inbound Trade Mission made all the difference. Meeting in my home town reduced cost and time and meant I could bake fresh samples and personally present my products.

'While working with distributors in Asia is essential, it is difficult to present products personally to major retailers and we are not always fully aware of all the in-market and competitive factors.'
 

'Having my range profiled in the FIAL eCatalogue meant that the buyer pre-selected products of most appeal for their customers and this gives the buyer meeting a higher chance of success. I keep my profile updated and hope to be connected to more export buyers.

'My product was also featured in the Park'n Shop Australian promotion that utilises FIAL POS designs on gondola ends, posters, shelf talkers and in online advertising and in retail flyers. We will be watching closely for the impact on sales.'
