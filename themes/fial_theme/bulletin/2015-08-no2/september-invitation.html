Background

The AFGC is hosting an Industry Briefing in relation to a scoping study on Food Safety Auditing. The study will assist industry collaboration in addressing food safety auditing duplication.

Retailers, quick service restaurants, food service companies and food manufacturers commission a large number of food safety and quality audits each year. These are in addition to enforcement agencies conducting food safety audits. There is appreciable overlap with companies being audited against different standards with similar requirements multiple times, representing a significant burden in cost and time.

Discussions coordinated by the AFGC have been ongoing to determine how the industry can collectively work together to address this issue whilst at the same time maintaining food safety outcomes across all sectors.

Specifically the current study will:

  • collect and collate the many auditing standards which are currently being used across industry sectors with a view to streamlining their food safety provisions in future work;
  • establish the key attributes of effective food safety management within companies;
  • determine the cost burden of multiple audits; and
  • identify the major stakeholder groups and establish mechanisms for communicating with them for future consultations on food safety auditing issues.

Industry Briefing

The purpose of the Industry Briefing is to secure support for future work on reducing audit costs and improving food safety outcomes across the industry.
