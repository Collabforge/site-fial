A new tool for Australian food export success in Asia
 

The Export Market Insights database, or EMI, captures a wealth of export information from multiple leading data sources in one location to support exporters' decisions.

The database provides direct links to information sources, industry reports as well as 304 individual product-country reports across 38 product categories within eight key Asian markets.
 
 

Click here to access the EMI and find out more.

The EMI has been developed in collaboration with the ISCL (Institute for Supply Chain and Logistics) at Victoria University.

22 projects in 2014 deliver results for business

The 22 technical projects undertaken in the 2014 SME Solution Centre pilot program are now nearing completion and are reporting overwhelming success.

The collaboration between business and researcher experts at CSIRO, DIAL and at Cooper's Plains facility of DAFF in Queensland has been highly effective and it's estimated that the technical solutions generated could yield an estimated $30 million once commercialised.

The projects have covered a diverse range of technical challenges across the whole value chain from new crops, sustainability to meal solutions and ingredients.

 

At least two companies are currently scaling up production to commercialise sales into new domestic retail as well as Asian markets. One company is now placed to develop an entirely new ingredient segment.

Several companies have now seen such value that they have committed their own funding for ongoing projects, beyond the matched funding for original projects.

One key component in the program has been training, which is vital to support the technical solution with expertise.
 
 
 

In November, FIAL hosted a buying mission for T.Choithram & Sons as a part of the very successful Inbound Buyer Mission Program. Choithram is a major food retailer and distributor across several Middle East markets.

The Senior Procurement Manager for Choithram selected 60 companies to meet from the FIAL eCatalogue and with the support of the State Government agencies held one-on-one meetings in Perth, Adelaide, Melbourne, Sydney and Brisbane.

Preliminary results from the inbound mission are very positive;

  • 90% of the buyer/supplier meetings were new connections

 
  • 60% of the meetings are likely to result in a trial order from the buyer, pending negotiations
  • 50% of suppliers received an overall liking score of 4/5 or 5/5 for their product and range

Given the success of FIAL's program, we are planning to host the next group of senior buyers in the first quarter of 2015.

If you are interested in securing a one-on-one appointment with Senior Buyers from leading high-end supermarket chains and distributors please register your supplier profile now on the FIAL eCatalogue.
