I had the recent pleasure of attending both the NSW and Queensland Premier's Export Awards, at which FIAL sponsored the Agribusiness Awards. I was immensely impressed by the nominated Australian food and agribusiness enterprises and their exceptional commitment to achieving export success.
 
Entering new markets requires innovation at every step of the value chain. Insight and adaptation is required for different consumers, tastes, cultural considerations, languages and packaging as well as distribution channels and seller relationships. Businesses will undoubtedly face many challenges, but what all the Export Award winners showed was the drive and determination to confront obstacles head on. We must be innovative if we want to be global players.
 
On behalf of FIAL, I would like to congratulate the Queensland winner Australian Agricultural Company and special award winner CO YO, and the NSW winners, SunRice, regional winner Bega Cheese, and JBS and Atron Enterprises who were highly commended. Well done!
 
Peter Schutz
Chairman
 
At FIAL, we are working to deliver programs that provide you with genuine value. We have found that companies are more responsive and willing to invest in innovation when they see tangible benefits from an opportunity. The SME Solution Centre is a good example that demonstrates businesses clearly see significant value in R&D collaboration because each business matched FIAL's investment in this pilot.
 
We have also supported industry partner initiatives that deliver tangible benefits, such as the VCSCM industry forums seeking practical solutions to water and waste management, and engagement with the PMA and the Fresh Produce Safety Centre where sector wide research priorities to address market needs were identified.
 
The wonderful response to the pilot market workshops on channel diversification, export, retail and food service readiness, shows that we have hit the mark in terms of developing initiatives to address industry needs. Once again, thank you for your tremendous support and feedback on how we can continue to deliver programs that make a real difference for you and your business.
 
Mirjana Prica
Managing Director

  FOOD FUTURES DELIVERS BUSINESS BENEFITS

The Food Futures programs for innovation, training and development are well underway with positive feedback coming from the 50+ participating companies.

Those in the Food Futures Clusters programs have highlighted its benefits for small and medium companies. In addition to learning new tools and processes they are establishing new peer networks and integrating collaboration into their innovation process, and some have already identified low cost productivity initiatives that deliver value to the bottom line.
 
The Food Futures Leaders in Innovation program is focused on providing critical leadership skills for people looking to transition from technical roles to become leaders in innovation. The program also helps managers develop wider skills to lead innovation in their companies.
 
We are in the process of rolling out a pilot for online collaboration. Stay tuned.
 
More information on Foods Future Leader in Innovation can be found here.

The FIAL eCatalogue has been launched giving Australian food and beverage producers greater access to overseas buyers and opportunities to expand their supply chains beyond Australia.
﻿﻿﻿﻿
The first-of-its-kind interactive database is designed for overseas buyers and lists Australian suppliers along with key information about their export history, unique selling proposition, and product range. More than 350 Australian food and beverage producers are already listed.
 
While targeting overseas businesses as its primary users, the database's analytics also will help the Australian food and beverage industry to recognise opportunities, product demands and emerging export trends.
