On Tuesday 17th February 2015, Industry Minister Ian Macfarlane announced the Chairs to lead the government's industry growth centres initiative (see link below). I am pleased to say that I have been extended the privilege to be announced as the Chair of the new Food and Agribusiness Growth Centre, which is one of five key sectors of competitive advantage as identified by the government in their Industry Innovation and Competitiveness Agenda. Over the next months we will engage broadly across the food and agribusiness sector to develop the proposal for the growth centre. The centres will leverage experience and expertise across the sector to catalyse the formation of collaborative partnerships between industry, research and government to develop practical pathways to grow, boost productivity, reduce the regulatory burden and create jobs.

I believe the Federal Government's vote of confidence in food and agribusiness recognises the important work we at FIAL and others in the industry have already done to raise the awareness of the sector's contribution towards the Australian economy. Through the centre, all efforts will be concentrated on continuing to build on the work of FIAL and others in accelerating Australia's capability and capacity in local and export markets.

FIAL chairman Peter Schutz returned from Australia's largest business trip to India in January with some invaluable tips for SME's from India's most influential retailers and industry representatives.

Peter said the trip clarified the significance of India as an important export market to rival China, with a quarter of a billion Indians classified as 'middle class' and population growth forecast to outstrip China's in 10 years.
 

The Australian delegation was told to follow the three P's - Partner, Promote and Persevere - to maximise its share of Indian food imports, growing at 30% per annum, and to tap into India's organised food and grocery market which is expected to grow from US$9 billion to US$34 billion by 2016.

Addressing the supplier need for in-market support and buyer need for effective Australian product messaging in-store, The Sultan Center Kuwait piloted point of sale material designed by FIAL for their Australia product in-store promotion. New Website Connects F&B Companies for Success in USA

United States Connect provides a one-stop-shop for Australian food & beverage companies to access the information, advice and contacts they need to succeed in the United States.

The U.S. remains the world's largest economy and by far the biggest consumer market. While its size means the States can be a very profitable, it is also highly competitive and has one of the highest 'failure' rates for market entrants. This was confirmed by Australia's International Business Survey, which ranked the U.S. as the top market for Australian exporters but also the third most difficult market to do business in.

For more information on United States Connect and to register go to www.unitedstatesconnect.com.
