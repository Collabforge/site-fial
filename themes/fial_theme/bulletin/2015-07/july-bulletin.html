I am delighted to inform you that Minister for Industry and Science, Ian Macfarlane, has approved our Food and Agribusiness Growth Centre proposal which is part of the $225 million Industry Growth Centres Initiative. FIAL will begin delivering its strategy to drive productivity, competitiveness and excellence in the food and agribusiness sector.

The four main areas of focus will be reducing regulatory burden, commercialising new products and services, engaging with global markets and supply chains, and improving workforce skills. FIAL will receive $15.4m from the Australian Government for the first four years of its operation as a Growth Centre, and look to increase this investment from industry and others sources.

The new Growth Centre Board also met for the first time on the 29th June 2015 where various strategic issues relating to the food and agribusiness sector were discussed. Details about the forthcoming sectoral strategy that will be used to align the Growth Centre activities will be shared over the coming year.

On behalf of the FIAL team and I, we are honoured to be given this privilege to be part of the team that will help to strengthen Australia’s global competitive position and in turn the sectors’ contribution towards the Australian economy.

The team and I are also excited, as we are able to take many of our pilot initiatives to the next stage of development to help the sector achieve its goals.

The Growth Centre headquarters will be located at the CSIRO’s Food Innovation Centre in Werribee, Victoria. FIAL will be able to leverage and build onto CSIRO’s extensive national network of business and researchers to improve the engagement between researchers and industry to encourage innovation across the value chain. It will also ensure that businesses from across Australia can participate in and benefit from the other activities of the Growth Centre.

I would like to take this opportunity to express my sincere gratitude to La Trobe University for kindly hosting FIAL for the past two years. Their tremendous support and cooperation in helping us to establish the organisation has allowed FIAL to get on with the task of helping the food and beverage industry. FIAL would not have been able to deliver the many programmes and initiatives of relevance to the industry without their support. FIAL is very much looking forward to continue to work with La Trobe University and the ‘Food North’ Steering Committee.

Expressions of interests are welcome from companies to participate in ‘The Enterprise Solution Centre’. This program, previously referred to as the SME Solution Centre, is now being funded by both CSIRO and FIAL. The program connects companies to technical and/or research advice and expertise to provide a solution to a technical challenge businesses are unable to solve themselves in order to take advantage of a market opportunity. The new solution, when supported with training that builds business capability, is designed to increase the competitiveness of the company in their desired markets. A 1:1 matched company funding is a requirement to participate.

To date, FIAL has partnered with more than 20 companies on research projects of varying scale which have the potential to deliver >$40M in sales once launched.

Building on the success of the FIAL eCatalogue, we have launched the fresh food supplier profile for Australian fruit and vegetable producers.

Suppliers can showcase their products to over 400 pre‐qualified international buyers. In collaboration with leading fruit and vegetable distributors in Asia, Australian Horticulture Exporters’ Association, Austrade and the Victorian Department of Environment and Primary Industries, the profile developed lists key company and produce information to aid international buyers sourcing decisions including Exclusive Varieties, Farming Capability, Certifications & Accreditations and Unique Selling Proposition.

This is a free service for Australian suppliers and international buyers. To register, please visit www.australianfoodcatalogue.com.au or contact Mr Najib Lawand, GM Market Development on najib.lawand@fial.com.au.
