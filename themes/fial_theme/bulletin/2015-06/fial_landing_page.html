BULLETIN: JUNE 2015

Message from Peter

I am delighted to inform you that we have submitted the Food and Agribusiness Growth Centre Application to Minister Ian Macfarlane. These past few months have been busy collecting your feedback and using this to inform the Application. We believe the industry is well placed to take advantage of the many identified opportunities locally and overseas.

I am also pleased to say that on Tuesday 2nd June, Minister MacFarlane announced the four new external Board members for FIAL who will join me as Chair and Mannaging Director, Dr Mirjana Prica as Executive Director. They are Simplot Managing Director & AFGC Chairman Terry O'Brien, CSIRO Director and dairy industry expert Jane Bennett, CSU Chancellor, food science research and commercialisation expert Dr Michele Allan and Geoff Starr, Director of food relief charity Foodbank Australia and Australian Pork Ltd.

The diverse background of the Board will bring a range of business know-how covering SME to MNEs, business-research collaborations and experience with export market development.

I am very much looking forward to working with the new Board in helping the industry to grow. One of our first priorities is to develop the Competitiveness Strategy for the Food & Agribusiness sector. Further details about the press release can be found here

We will keep you informed on the progress of the Application as soon as any information becomes available.

 

Message from Mirjana

The consultation process and the meetings with the 300 plus industry stakeholders have provided strong insights into the challenges facing the industry and also refined our thinking into how we overcome them. The overwhelming support and responses from the industry have been tremendous and we are very excited at the next phase of our journey.

On behalf of the team and I, we would like to thank you for your continuing encouragement and contribution in helping our industry transform.

Please email us today to receive a full copy of the Consultation Report.

NEW: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY GUIDE

The different types of intellectual property (IP) can include product formulation, manufacturing set-up, product format or even a brand. Often companies cut corners on protecting their IP and then can run into trouble particularly, as their product and markets expand.

FIAL has developed, in partnership with Cullens Patent and Trade Mark Attorneys, an introductory IP Guide outlining what businesses need to consider and what steps to take to protect their IP.

MARKET NEWS

HOFEX STAND CREATES HUGE INTEREST

Najib Lawand, FIAL's General Manager of Market Development, said the Australian stand created 'a massive stir', with a large number of buyers - and competing exhibitors - coming into the welcoming stand, evaluating the product displayed, commenting on the effective Point of Sale design and sharing positive feedback on the relevance and practicality of the FIAL eCatalogue.

A total of 250 qualified buyers registered for the eCatalogue at the show, with most based in the Hong Kong and China markets but also from Thailand, Malaysia, Russia and Latin America. The diversity of the high quality products displayed at the stand provided the incentive for buyers to view over 450 Australian supplier profiles available on the eCatalogue. The majority of buyers were importers and distributors but a fair share of retailers, hospitality and eCommerce platform buyers have registered on the eCatalogue.

HOFEX 2015 was the largest show in its 16-year history, with 65,000 m2 exhibiting space providing a platform for 2,400 international exhibitors to connect with 40,000 regional buyers.

"With so many active buyers registered on the eCatalogue, the time is ripe for export-oriented food and beverage suppliers to profile their products on the eCatalogue. Experience shows that buyers are most active in viewing categories of interest within the first month of registering", Najib advised.

To register on the catalogue, suppliers simply go www.australianfoodcatalogue.com.au and click on the supplier option.

NEW SERIES OF EXPORT READINESS WORKSHOPS

In collaboration with Xpotential, Export Council of Australia and State Government Agencies, these workshops focus on detailed market and buyer insights, market entry strategies, branding in market and the export value chain while also covering finance, legal and logistics topics. The presenters from Xpotential have over 40 years export experience managing international sales based both from Australia and in market.

Najib Lawand, General Manager Market Development says: "We have innovated how training workshops are delivered by online technology throughout the workshop. The participants will leave with an export market plan that incorporates their self-assessed strengths and weaknesses as highlighted throughout the day. This then gives the participant to focus on the areas that needs most work to become export ready."

Workshops have already been run in Gold Coast, Melbourne and Sydney with attendee feedback rating the workshops with near perfect scores on relevance, content and delivery. Remaining workshops are in Manjimup on 11th June and Perth on 12th June 2015. More workshops are being planned for the next quarter and include locations in regional Queensland, NSW and Victoria.

Register your interest to attend the 'Are you ready for export?' workshops through info@fial.com.au

EXPORT MARKET INSIGHTS

Food companies asked us to drill down to the level of their specific categories such as yoghurt rather than the higher level dairy. In response to this request, we have added 19 extra categories for each of the following key markets: China, Thailand, Malaysia, Japan, Indonesia, Hong Kong, Vietnam and South Korea.

The Export Market Insights or EMI database brings multiple data sources together into meaningful reports to help exporting companies identify trends, opportunities, competitors or emerging country markets.

The EMI also gives you links to the source data and provides information on import processed and requirements by country.

This very useful tool has the top 5 retailer websites by country so you can view your competitor product range and pricing in real time.

As exporting companies we encourage you to explore the EMI
